Travel time to moon
Due to regulations by the UNSC (United Nations Space Command) and other signatories, speeds are required to be reduced between the Earth and the Moon. As such, it takes approximately 3 to 4 hours.
Travel time to mars
Mars is the last stopping point for mining ships heading into the main asteroid belt, as well as a midpoint for mining drones making the long trip to the Kuiper Belt. While Titan is planned to take on most of the drone traffic in the coming decades, for now the space around Mars is rather crowded. As such, plan on 8-12 hours of travel.
Mars safety with Secunda Domus Project
While certain areas of Mars are off limits, the heavy industry and pollution is intentional. As we continue to clean up our planet from the disasters of the 21st century, the UNC (United Nations Coalition) as well as the few nation-states remaining have agreed to transfer all Co2 producing industries off world to Mars. What is bad for Earth is great for Mars, as over time the excess Co2 will help in our terraforming project. But Unity is well protected with multiple fail safes, as are your complimentary enviro-suits.
Zero Gravity Travel
Thankfully, as of last year solit upgraded our entire fleet to the new S-848. Not only is it faster than the S-800, all passenger cabins have a low-level gravity (slightly less than the Moons) as well as magboots. For those who still struggle with the reduced gravity we do still offer the complimentary SOLIT Enhanced THC supplements to help with nausea and to provide an overall wonderful experience.
Travel Time to Europa/Titan
Approximate travel time to Europa is 2 days, while Titan is an estimated 4 days. Thankfully, once past the main asteroid belt the S-848 is able to run its sub-hyper drive at 100% power. This allows us to shorten your trip there and back from what just a few decades ago took weeks!
SOLIT Future Plans
The S-900's hyperdrive has shown great promise in coming extremely close to achieving lightspeed. At this time we are unable to share more as this is a joint-venture with the UNCN, but we can share that the human trials have shown promise and that most test subjects have shown little to no physical effects from traveling at such high speeds.

Extra solar travel
As our understanding of quantum physics and mechanics grows, we're learning that there are ways to travel faster than the speed of light. Of course, "faster than the speed of light" is an incredibly incorrect way to put it, but the physics of "FTL" drives is rather difficult! We now know that "FTL" travel is possible in theory, but currently lack the ability to generate the amount of energy needed to even test the theory. We are hoping that this next century is as productive or more than the last century.
