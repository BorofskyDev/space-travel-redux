Why does it take so long to get to the moon?

Due to regulations by the UNSC (United Nations Space Command) and other signatories, speeds are required to be reduced between the Earth and the Moon. As such, it takes aproximately 3 to 4 hours.

Why have travel times to Mars increased?

Mars is the last stopping point for mining ships heading into the main asteroid belt, as well as a midpoint for mining drones making the long trip to the Kuiper Belt. While Titan is planned to take on most of the drone traffic in the coming decades, for now the space around Mars is rather crowded. As such, plan on 8-12 hours of travel.

Is it safe to visit Mars with the Secunda Domus Project ongoing

Yes! While certain areas of Mars are off limits, the heavy industry and pollution is intentional. As we continue to clean up our planet from the disasters of the 21st century, the UNC (United Nations Coalition) as well as the few nation-states remaining have agreed to transfer all Co2 producing industries off world to Mars. What is bad for Earth is great for Mars, as over time the excess Co2 will help in our terraforming project. But Unity is well protected with multiple failsafes, as are your complimentary envirosuits.

Zero Gravity makes me sick. Should I avoid traveling to Europa or Titan?

Thankfully, as of last year SOLIT upgraded our entire fleet to the new S-848. Not only is it faster than the S-800, all passenger cabins have a low-level gravity (slightly less than the Moons) as well as magboots. For those who still struggle with the reduced gravity we do still offer the complimentary Enhanced THC supplements to help with nausea and to provide an overall wonderful experience.

How long does it take to get to Europa and Titan?

Aproximate travel time to Europa is 2 days, while Titan is an estimated 3 days. Thankfully, once past the main asteroid belt the S-848 is able to run its sub-hyper drive at 100% power. This allows us to shorten your trip there and back from what just a few decades ago took weeks!

Does SOLIT have any plans to go further?

Yes! The UNC has granted SOLIT rights to a hotel on Neptune's upcoming Cecilia Payne Observatory. The science quarters are expected to be completed within five years, with the station being fully completed within the next ten years. This center will be used to launch probes past the Kuiper Belt to detect and study nearby star systems in our quest to one day travel outside of our solar system. Civilian travel to Neptune should commence in the next fiften years as we await the final testing and UNC approval of the SOLIT S-900.

Is it true SOLIT is producing a ship capable of the speed of light?

The S-900's hyperdrive has shown great promise in coming extremely close to achieving lightspeed. At this time we are unable to share more as this is a joint-venture with the UNCN, but we can share that the human trials have shown promise and that most test subjects have shown little to no physical effects from traveling at such high speeds.

Will we be able to travel to planets outwide our system?

As our understanding of quantum physics grows, we're learning that there are ways to travel faster than the speed of light. Of course, "faster than the speed of light" is an incredibly incorrect way to put it, but the physics of "FTL" drives is rather difficult! We now know that "FTL" travel is possible in theory, but currently lack the ability to generate the amount of energy needed to even test the theory. We are hoping that this next century is as productive or more than the last century.
